Product Vision Board

A strategic tool that helps teams align on the product vision, target users, needs, features, and business goals.

Board Components

Vision Statement

The change you want to see in the world

  • What's the ultimate purpose?
  • What's the positive change?
  • How will the world be different?

Target Group

Who are your users and customers?

  • User segments
  • Customer characteristics
  • Market demographics

Needs

What problems are you solving?

  • User pain points
  • Market gaps
  • Opportunities

Product

What are the key features?

  • Core functionality
  • Key differentiators
  • Value propositions

Business Goals

How will you measure success?

  • Revenue targets
  • Market share goals
  • Growth metrics

OKRs Framework

Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) is a goal-setting framework that helps organizations set challenging, ambitious goals with measurable results.

Framework Structure

Objectives

Qualitative, inspirational goals that provide direction

  • Ambitious
  • Qualitative
  • Time-bound
  • Actionable

Key Results

Quantitative metrics that measure progress

  • Measurable
  • Quantitative
  • Achievable
  • Verifiable

OKR Cycle

Set

Define objectives and key results

Align

Ensure team alignment

Track

Monitor progress regularly

Review

Evaluate and adjust

Porter's Five Forces

A framework for analyzing competition and industry structure to determine business strategy.

The Five Forces

Competitive Rivalry

Current competition in the market

Supplier Power

Bargaining power of suppliers

Buyer Power

Bargaining power of buyers

Threat of Substitution

Alternative products or services

Threat of New Entry

Potential new competitors

Framework Comparison

Framework Best For Time Horizon Complexity
Product Vision Board Product direction and alignment Long-term Low
Business Model Canvas Business model innovation Medium to Long-term Medium
OKRs Goal setting and execution Short to Medium-term Medium
Porter's Five Forces Industry analysis Long-term High
